LEARN TO MAKE SOIL MIX

watch the video or read the description below

Base Ingredients: Start with equal parts of garden soil and compost. Garden soil provides minerals and structure, while compost enriches the soil with nutrients and improves drainage.Add Organic Matter: Mix in additional organic matter such as well-rotted manure or leaf mold. This boosts soil fertility, encourages microbial activity, and enhances water retention.Aeration and Drainage: Incorporate perlite or coarse sand to improve aeration and drainage. These additives prevent soil compaction and ensure healthy root growth by maintaining proper oxygen levels.Optional Amendments: Depending on your plant preferences, consider adding amendments like bone meal for phosphorus, blood meal for nitrogen, or kelp meal for trace minerals. These supplements address specific nutrient needs and promote robust plant growth.Mix Thoroughly: Combine all ingredients in a large container or directly in the garden bed. Use a shovel or garden fork to mix the components thoroughly, ensuring uniform distribution.Test Soil: Before planting, conduct a soil test to assess pH levels and nutrient content. Adjust the soil mix as needed based on the test results to create an optimal growing environment for your kitchen garden.By following these steps, you can prepare a nutrient-rich soil mix tailored to the needs of your kitchen garden, fostering healthy plant growth and abundant harvests.

Tomatoes: Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost date or purchase seedlings. Transplant seedlings outdoors once the soil has warmed and all danger of frost has passed. Plant in well-drained soil enriched with compost, and provide support for vines as they grow. Water consistently, aiming to keep the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Stake or cage tomato plants to support heavy fruiting and prevent sprawling. Peppers: Start seeds indoors 8-10 weeks before the last frost date or purchase seedlings. Transplant seedlings outdoors after the soil has warmed and all danger of frost has passed. Plant in fertile, well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Water regularly, ensuring the soil stays consistently moist but not soggy. Provide support for taller pepper varieties to prevent bending or breaking.
